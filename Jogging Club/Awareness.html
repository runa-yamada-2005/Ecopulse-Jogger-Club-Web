JOGGING IS MORE THAN MOVEMENT

Jogging isn’t just movement — it’s a way to notice.

Every step outdoors brings you closer to the world around you: the birds in the trees, the breeze on your skin, the scent of fresh leaves after rain.

At our club, we call this mindful jogging — tuning into your surroundings while staying active.

WHAT YOU MIGHT NOTICE

  • Sounds – bird calls, rustling leaves, water flowing nearby
  • Scents – flowers, rain-soaked earth, morning air
  • Sights – blooming trees, shifting light, wildlife on the trail
  • Feel – uneven paths, soft grass, wind brushing past

WHY IT MATTERS

Jogging in nature improves mood, focus, and creativity — and builds appreciation for the environment. When you slow down and notice, every jog becomes a small act of connection.

CLUB INITIATIVES THAT PROMOTE NATURE AWARENESS

  • Jog & Clean Events: Jog while collecting litter on trails
  • Tree Spotting Walks: Learn local tree names and their stories
  • Leave No Trace Guide: Practical tips for eco-friendly jogging
  • Mindful Jogging Days: Jog in silence, observe and reflect

MINI NATURE SPOTTING CHECKLIST

Try to spot at least 3 of these during your next jog:

Hear birds singing
Smell flowers or fresh grass
See a butterfly or bee
Feel the wind on your skin
Notice a tree or plant you’ve never really looked at before

TIP: JOG SLOWER OR PAUSE FOR A MOMENT - NATURE REVEALS ITSELF TO THOSE WHO PAY ATTENTION.
