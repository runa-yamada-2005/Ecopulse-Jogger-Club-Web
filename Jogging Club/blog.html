BLOG

The Nature Jog Blog is a space for members to share personal reflections and stories from their jogging experiences, focusing on quiet thoughts, surprising moments, and lessons learned rather than training tips or route guides.

Jogging often provides a moment of solitude and clarity, allowing runners to process thoughts, generate ideas, and notice small details they might otherwise overlook.

Blog entries capture a variety of experiences, from the feeling of rain and wind during a run to observing new flowers or familiar animals along the path. Each post blends movement, mindfulness, and the environment, fostering a community where joggers inspire one another to not only move but also to observe, reflect, and connect with the world around them.

This week, I laced up a little earlier than usual. The sky was still sleepy, soft grey with the promise of light. The grass shimmered with dew, and the world felt hushed — like it was holding its breath before the day began. About ten minutes into my jog, just as I rounded the curve near the old sycamore, I caught a glimpse of something small darting across the path — a meadow hare. It paused momentarily, ears alert, eyes watching me as if deciding whether I was part of the peace or a threat to it. I slowed, not wanting to disturb its moment. We stood like that — still — two morning wanderers sharing the same breath of misty air. It struck me then how rarely I stop. Not just physically, but mentally. Most days are a race through tasks and routines. But that moment — eyes meeting a hare’s in the hush of dawn — felt like a reset. It reminded me that nature isn’t a backdrop to my run. It is the run. The hare eventually hopped away into the underbrush, and I continued. But the rest of the jog felt lighter somehow. More connected. As if I was running with the morning, not just through it. I didn’t track my pace today. I didn’t need to. Some runs aren’t about time — they’re about presence. Until next week,
– Amelia

Finding Joy
in the Little
Things During
a Run

-17/6/2025-

 

Jogging has always been more than just a form of exercise for me—it’s a way to reconnect with the world around me. Recently, I took a run in the rain, and it was a refreshing experience. The raindrops lightly tapped my skin, cooling me down and bringing a sense of calm that’s hard to find elsewhere. The wind brushed gently across my face, making me feel alive and awake, as if nature itself was encouraging me to keep going. On my usual path, I noticed a small flower blooming where there hadn’t been one before. It was a simple detail, but it reminded me to slow down and appreciate the subtle changes happening around me every day. These little moments are often overlooked in the rush of life, but when you’re running, your senses sharpen, and you begin to notice the beauty in the smallest things. As I continued, I passed by the same old wall where a cat always sits. Today, it was there again, watching me with curious eyes. That silent companionship added a comforting familiarity to my run, as if the cat and I shared a quiet understanding of the world passing by. Running in the rain, spotting new blooms, and exchanging glances with a familiar cat—these are the moments that make my runs special. They remind me that running isn’t just about physical fitness; it’s about being present, noticing the world, and finding joy in the simple, everyday wonders.
